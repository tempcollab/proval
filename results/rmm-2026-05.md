# rmm-2026-05

Triangle ABC with AB < AC, O circumcentre, XYZT a parallelogram inside ABC with
∠AXB = ∠AZC, ∠AZB = ∠AXC, ∠AYB = ∠ATC, ∠ATB = ∠AYC. Prove the diagonals XZ and YT
intersect on the circumcircle of triangle BOC.

## Status
partial

## Approaches tried
- Reduce angle hypotheses to circle + cross-ratio conditions — WORKED (verified
  numerically to 1e-15). The pair (∠AXB=∠AZC, ∠AZB=∠AXC) is equivalent to:
  (a) B,C,X,Z concyclic (call ω₁), via ∠BXC = ∠BZC obtained by ADDING the two angle
  equalities (∠AXB+∠AXC = ∠AZB+∠AZC) with the inside-triangle branch; and
  (b) the cross-ratio K(X,Z) = (A−X)(A−Z)/[(B−X)(C−Z)] is real, equivalent to
  f(X)+f(Z)=0 where f(P)=∠APB−∠APC (obtained by SUBTRACTING the two equalities).
  Same for the (Y,T) pair: B,C,Y,T concyclic (ω₂) and K(Y,T) ∈ ℝ. Parallelogram
  XYZT ⟺ X+Z = Y+T = 2M (common diagonal midpoint).
- Unsigned-angle brute-force search for valid parallelograms — DEAD END as a
  verification method. The UNSIGNED angle conditions admit many spurious branches
  (X,Z,Y,T on wrong arcs); brute-force solutions give parallelograms whose midpoint
  M is NOT on circ(BOC) (cross-ratio-imag ≈ 0.3 typically, never near 0). The
  theorem holds only for the intended inside-triangle (directed) configuration. Any
  proof MUST use the directed/inside-triangle branch, encoded as K(·,·) ∈ ℝ plus
  same-arc concyclicity — NOT arbitrary unsigned-angle solutions.
- "Midpoint of a single valid chord lies on circ(BOC)" — FALSE (verified).
  Midpoints of single (X,Z) pairs fill a 2-D region. circ(BOC) membership requires
  the COUPLING of the two chords XZ and YT through the shared midpoint M.

## Current best
The hypotheses are equivalent (verified 1e-15) to:
  (i)   B,C,X,Z concyclic on ω₁ and K(X,Z)=(A−X)(A−Z)/[(B−X)(C−Z)] ∈ ℝ;
  (ii)  B,C,Y,T concyclic on ω₂ and K(Y,T)=(A−Y)(A−T)/[(B−Y)(C−T)] ∈ ℝ;
  (iii) X+Z = Y+T = 2M  (parallelogram).
Goal reduces to: M lies on circ(BOC) ⟺ (with O=0 on the unit circumcircle, |a|=|b|
=|c|=1) (M−b)c/[(M−c)b] ∈ ℝ ⟺ ∠BMC = 2∠A.

The concyclicity lemma (L1) and the cross-ratio invariant (L2, with the precise
grouping K = (A−X)(A−Z)/[(B−X)(C−Z)] pinned down against alternatives) are
established (machine-checked to 1e-12 over 40 genuine inside-triangle pairs). These
are the proven partial progress.

### Round-6 outline-review diagnosis (RETHINK — spine refuted)
The intended elimination spine ("reduce to (i)+(ii)+(iii), then eliminate to force M
on circ(BOC)") targets a FALSE intermediate statement and CANNOT be built as written:
- "M is the shared midpoint of two K-real concyclic chords" describes a 2-D region of
  M almost disjoint from circ(BOC). A generic M off circ(BOC) admits MANY (7,15,22,…,
  150+) K-real concyclic chords with midpoint M; points on circ(BOC) are not special
  this way. So no resultant/radical-axis finish can output circ(BOC) from this system.
- WHY the K∈ℝ encoding does not save it: K∈ℝ is the SYMMETRIC condition f(X)+f(Z)=0,
  which is invariant under the very arc/endpoint reflections that generate the spurious
  branches. The lost information is the ANTISYMMETRIC part f(X)−f(Z).
- A corrected strategy must supply a DIRECTED invariant that is NOT reflection-invariant,
  and must first numerically construct a genuine full XYZT configuration by continuation
  (random sampling hits 0 valid configs in 8000 trials) before any elimination.
DEFERRED: needs a new spine (directed antisymmetric invariant). L1/L2 stand as the
recorded partial.
